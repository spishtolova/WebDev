Tips for the best macarons

  • Work Fast with Ice Cream : Keep the ice cream extra frozen before assembly to prevent melting while spreading the meringue.
  • Use a Kitchen Torch for Precision : While the oven works, a torch gives better control and avoids melting the ice cream.
  • Make Ahead for Convenience : You can freeze the assembled Baked Alaska (without toasting the meringue) for up to 2 days before serving!

Baked Alaska: A Show-Stopping Frozen Delight

Baked Alaska is the ultimate dessert for those who love both fire and ice! This spectacular treat features a layer of cake, a dome of ice cream, and a fluffy meringue topping, all toasted to golden perfection.

A dessert that’s as dramatic as it is delicious! Baked Alaska is the perfect balance of hot and cold—fluffy, golden-browned meringue hiding layers of creamy ice cream and soft cake. The magic happens when it’s torched or baked, creating a caramelized shell that crackles with every bite.

The contrast between the cold ice cream and the warm, caramelized meringue makes every bite a magical experience. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just want to impress, this Baked Alaska is sure to steal the show!

Instructions

Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and line a 9-inch round cake pan. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, and salt.

In another bowl, beat butter and sugar until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, then mix in vanilla. Alternate adding dry ingredients and milk, mixing until just combined.Pour batter into the pan and bake for 20–25 minutes. Let cool completely.

Shape The Cake:

Line a medium-sized bowl with plastic wrap. Soften the ice cream slightly and scoop it into the bowl, pressing it in firmly. Smooth the top, cover, and freeze for at least 4 hours or overnight.

Place the cooled cake on a serving plate. Unmold the frozen ice cream dome and place it on top of the cake.

Make the Meringue:

Beat egg whites and cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar and continue beating until stiff, glossy peaks form. Mix in vanilla.

Spread the meringue over the ice cream and cake, sealing it completely. Use a kitchen torch to lightly brown the meringue OR bake in a 220°C (425°F) oven for 3–5 minutes until golden.

Slice and serve right away, or freeze for later—just let it sit at room temperature for 5–10 minutes before slicing.

Final Thoughts

Baked Alaska is the perfect dessert for making a statement! The warm, toasty meringue and the cold, creamy ice cream create a contrast that’s both delicious and visually stunning. Whether it’s a birthday, holiday, or just a fancy treat, this dessert is sure to impress.

Ingredients:

For the base (cake layer):

  • 120g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 115g unsalted butter (softened)
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 120ml whole milk

For the ice cream layer:

  • 1L of your favorite ice cream (vanilla, chocolate, or a mix)

For the meringue:

  • 3 large egg whites
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
