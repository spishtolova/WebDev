Tips for the best macarons

  • For a more stable meringue, separate the egg whites and let them sit at room temperature for 24h before using.
  • When folding the dry ingredients, use the “figure-eight” test: The batter should flow smoothly off the spatula and form a figure-eight without breaking.
  • After piping, let the macarons sit at room temperature for 30–45 minutes until a thin, dry skin forms. This prevents cracking and helps develop the macaron feet when baked.

Delicate Lavender Macarons: A Floral Twist on a Classic Treat

There’s something enchanting about the delicate flavor of lavender in a macaron. The floral notes pair beautifully with the light, crisp shell and creamy filling, making each bite feel like a little piece of Parisian elegance.

Delicate, airy, and irresistibly charming—lavender macarons are like tiny bites of sophistication. The first time I made them, I was captivated by their pastel hue and the floral aroma that filled my kitchen. There’s something almost magical about them, knowing that soon they’ll be sandwiched with a luscious filling.

If you love subtle floral flavors and elegant desserts, these Lavender Macarons are a must-try!

Instructions

Sift the almond flour, powdered sugar, and ground lavender together. Set aside.

Whisk egg whites with cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add granulated sugar and whip to stiff peaks. If using, mix in a tiny amount of purple gel food coloring.

Fold the dry ingredients into the meringue gently until the batter flows smoothly. Pipe small circles onto a lined baking sheet. Tap the tray to release air bubbles. Let them rest for 30–45 minutes until a skin forms.

Bake at 150°C (300°F) for 12–14 minutes. Let cool completely.

Make the Buttercream Filling:

In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and butter together until smooth and creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, beating until fully incorporated. Stir in the vanilla extract and salt. /p>

Beat butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla until smooth. Add the lavender-infused milk and mix until fluffy. Pipe the buttercream onto one macaron shell and sandwich with another. Let the macarons rest in the fridge for 24 hours before enjoying for the best texture and flavor!

Final Thoughts

These Lavender Macarons are perfect for tea parties, special occasions, or simply treating yourself to a bit of floral indulgence. The subtle lavender pairs beautifully with the sweet almond shell, creating a sophisticated and delicate dessert.

Ingredients:

For the macaron shells:

  • 100g almond flour
  • 100g powdered sugar
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp dried culinary lavender (ground into a fine powder)
  • Purple gel food coloring (optional)
For the lavender buttercream filling:
  • 100g unsalted butter (softened)
  • 200g powdered sugar
  • 1 tbsp heavy cream
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp dried culinary lavender (steeped in 1 tbsp warm milk, then strained)
