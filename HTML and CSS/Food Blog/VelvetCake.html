Tips for the best Red Velvet Cake

  • Use Buttermilk for a Soft, Moist Crumb: Buttermilk is key to achieving that tender texture.
  • Don’t Overmix the Batter : Overmixing can make the cake dense. Stir until the ingredients are just combined to keep it light and fluffy.
  • Chill the Cake Before Frosting : For cleaner, smoother frosting, let the cake layers cool completely, then chill them in the fridge for 30 minutes before assembling.

Classic Red Velvet Cake: A Timeless Indulgence

Few cakes are as iconic as Red Velvet Cake—its deep crimson hue, soft crumb, and subtle cocoa flavor make it a showstopper for any occasion.

But what truly sets it apart is its velvety texture and the perfect balance of tangy and sweet flavors, thanks to a touch of buttermilk and a luscious cream cheese frosting.

There’s something undeniably magical about a slice of red velvet cake. With its striking crimson hue, velvety texture, and subtle hint of cocoa, it’s a true classic that feels both elegant and comforting. Every bite will in your mouth like a sweet, nostalgic embrace.

Whether you're celebrating a special moment or simply craving a decadent dessert, this homemade Red Velvet Cake is sure to impress!

Instructions

Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and line two 8-inch cake pans. In a bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.

Beat butter, sugar, and oil together until light and fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla and vinegar.

Alternate adding the dry ingredients and buttermilk in batches, mixing until just combined. Stir in the red food coloring.

Divide the batter evenly between the cake pans and bake for 25–30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Let cool completely.

Make the Frosting:

Beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla, mixing until fluffy.

Assemble the Cake:

Place one cake layer on a plate, spread frosting on top, then add the second layer and frost the entire cake. For the best flavor and texture, let the cake rest in the fridge for at least an hour before slicing.

Final Thoughts

This Red Velvet Cake is moist, flavorful, and beautifully balanced with the tangy cream cheese frosting. Whether it's for a birthday, holiday, or just a cozy treat, it's guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

For the cake:

  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 115g unsalted butter (softened)
  • 200g granulated sugar
  • 120ml vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • 240ml buttermilk
  • 1 tbsp red food coloring
For the cream cheese frosting:
  • 225g cream cheese (softened)
  • 115g unsalted butter (softened)
  • 300g powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
